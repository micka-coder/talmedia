
### Part 1
This teaches that you must <b>give a base to the altar of</b> the <b>burnt offering,</b> i.e., that the remainder of any blood placed on the altar must be poured on the base.

### Part 2
The Gemara explains: <b>Because if it enters your mind</b> that the verse states this simply to teach <b>as it is written,</b> concerning this offering alone, <b>why do I</b> need <b>these verses</b> with regard to the sin offering of a king? If you would answer: The verses are needed <b>to</b> teach the <i>halakha</i> that the <b>remainder</b> of the blood must be poured on the external altar rather than on the inner altar, then the question remains: Is there any need for the Torah to teach this about the <b>remainder? But</b> the sprinklings of blood themselves are <b>performed</b> on the <b>external</b> altar, so why would one think that the remainder of the blood should be poured on the inner altar?
