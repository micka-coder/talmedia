
### Part 1
<b>This</b> first <i>baraita</i> is in accordance with the opinion of <b>Rabbi Yishmael,</b> who derives that meat of a firstborn offering, which is an offering of lesser sanctity, cannot be consumed if the altar is damaged or absent, based upon the <i>halakha</i> pertaining to the blood of the firstborn. <b>That</b> second <i>baraita</i> is in accordance with the opinion of <b>the Sages,</b> who disagree with Rabbi Yishmael.

### Part 2
<b>And if you wish, say</b> there is a different resolution of the two <i>baraitot</i>: Both <b>this</b> <i>baraita</i> <b>and that</b> <i>baraita</i> are referring <b>to offerings of the most sacred order. And what</b> does the second <i>baraita</i> mean when it says the food may be consumed <b>in two locations?</b> It is referring to when the Israelites arrive at a new camp, <b>before the Levites erect the Tabernacle,</b>
