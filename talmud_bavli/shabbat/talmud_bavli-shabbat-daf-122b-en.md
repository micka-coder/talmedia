
### Part 1
<b>they kindle it with the majority</b> of the those present at the banquet <b>in mind.</b> When the majority of those present are gentiles, it is permitted.

### Part 2
The Gemara relates that <b>Shmuel happened</b> to come <b>to the house of Avin Toran. A certain gentile came</b> and <b>kindled a lamp. Shmuel turned his face back</b> away from the lamp in order to avoid benefitting from the light. <b>When</b> Shmuel <b>saw</b> that the gentile <b>brought a document and was reading</b> it, <b>he said: He kindled it with his</b> own benefit <b>in mind. He turned his face back toward the lamp.</b>
