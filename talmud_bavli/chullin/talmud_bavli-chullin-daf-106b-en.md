### Part 2
<b>Rav said</b> this <i>halakha</i> to his students while indicating with his hand the joints to which the <i>baraita</i> is referring: One washes <b>until here,</b> the second joint of the fingers, <b>for non-sacred</b> food, and <b>until there,</b> the third joint, where the fingers join the palm, <b>for <i>teruma</i>. And Shmuel</b> disagreed and <b>said:</b> One washes <b>until here both for</b> eating <b>non-sacred</b> food <b>and for <i>teruma</i>,</b> indicating <b>the more stringent</b> location, the third joint where the fingers join the palm. <b>And Rav Sheshet said: Until here both for</b> the consumption of <b>non-sacred</b> food <b>and for <i>teruma</i>,</b> indicating <b>the more lenient</b> location, the second joint.

### Part 3
<b>Bar Hedya said: I was standing before Rabbi Ami, and he said:</b> One washes <b>until here both for non-sacred</b> food <b>and for <i>teruma</i>,</b> indicating <b>the more stringent</b> location, the third joint. <b>And do not say</b> that <b>Rabbi Ami</b> acted this way only <b>because he is a priest</b> and acted stringently to avoid confusing <i>teruma</i> and non-sacred food. This cannot be, <b>as Rabbi Meyasha, son of the son of Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi, is a Levite, and</b> yet <b>he</b> too <b>said: Until here both for non-sacred</b> food <b>and for <i>teruma</i>,</b> indicating <b>the more stringent</b> location.
