It taxes the tree to sustain these blossoms, and this may render the tree incapable of sustaining the fruits that subsequently grow from the blossoms. Stones were used to weaken the tree during blossoming, thereby reducing the number of blossoms that it needed to nourish. <b>But</b> with regard to <b>painting it with red paint, for what</b> benefit is it performed that makes it permitted despite the fact that this was the practice of the Amorites? The Gemara explains: One does so <b>in order that people will see</b> the tree <b>and pray for it.</b>

### Part 2
<b>As it is taught</b> in a <i>baraita</i>: It is derived from the verse: <b>“And he will cry: Impure, impure”</b> (Leviticus 13:45), that a leper must publicize the fact that he is ritually impure. <b>He must announce</b> his pain <b>to the masses, and the masses will pray for mercy on his behalf. And likewise, one to whom</b> any unfortunate <b>matter happens must announce</b> it <b>to the masses, and</b> then <b>the masses will pray for mercy on his behalf.</b>

### Part 3
<b>Ravina said: In accordance with whose</b> opinion <b>do we hang bunches of</b> unripe <b>dates on a palm tree</b> that casts off its dates, despite the fact that this is the practice of the Amorites? It is <b>in accordance with</b> the opinion of <b>this <i>tanna</i></b> of the <i>baraita</i> just cited, who states that one must announce such occurrences to the masses so that they will pray for mercy.
