
### Part 1
<b>And had</b> the Torah <b>written</b> only: <b>“His head,” and not written: “His beard,” I would say</b> that “his head” <b>teaches two</b> matters. First, <b>that a positive</b> mitzva <b>comes and overrides a prohibition. And it</b> also <b>teaches that rounding the entire head is called rounding. And still, from where do we</b> derive that a leper must shave <b>with a razor? For this</b> reason <b>the Merciful One</b> also <b>wrote: “His beard,”</b> and we derive from the prohibition that bars priests from destroying their beards that the removal of the beard involves the use of a razor.

### Part 2
