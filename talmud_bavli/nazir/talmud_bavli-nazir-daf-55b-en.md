
### Part 1
<b>Rather, is it not</b> the case that the mishna is referring <b>to a lengthy naziriteship, and</b> nevertheless <b>it teaches: He starts counting immediately,</b> which indicates that his time as a leper is not included? Rav Sherevya <b>raised the objection and he resolved it:</b> The mishna is referring <b>to a naziriteship of fifty days,</b> in a case <b>where he sat</b> and observed <b>twenty</b> days of his vow, <b>and</b> at that point <b>he developed leprosy.</b> In that case, he <b>shaves</b> for <b>his leprosy, and he again sits</b> for <b>thirty days as a nazirite.</b> The problem of thirty days’ hair growth does not arise in this situation, <b>as</b> at the end of this period <b>there is hair growth</b> of thirty days.
