### passuk 4
<b>A traveler came.</b> [Noson] compared the evil inclination at first to a sojourner that is [quickly] going on his way. Afterwords he is compared to a guest that has become a resident and afterwords he is compared to a man who is the owner of the house.<sup>1</sup><i class="footnote">This verse is Noson’s parable alluding to the evil inclination. The visiton is first referred to as <span>הֵלֶךְ</span>, then <span>אׂרֵחַ</span> and finally as an <span>אִישׁ</span>, representing the evil inclination’s ability to take more and more control over its victim’s life.</i>

### passuk 30
<b>The crown of Malkom.</b> The abomination [idol] of the people of Ammon's name was Malkom. Denoting [the word] Moleich.<sup>10</sup><i class="footnote"><span>מוֹלֶךְ</span> is an idol to whom children were sacrificed.</i>
<b>It was [hung over] Dovid's head.</b> Itai the Gitite annulled it.<sup>11</sup><i class="footnote">This explanation of the verse is stated in Avodah Zoroh 24. It is prohibited for Jews to derive any pleasure from the idols of gentiles. They must be anulled by a gentile, i.e., taken out of the service of idol worship, after which they are permitted to Jews. Itai the Gitite, a gentile, did this to Malkom for Dovid’s sake.</i>

### passuk 31
<b>Saw-like utensils, iron threshing utensils and iron axes.</b> They are types of tortures.
<b>Saw-like utensils.</b> A knife serrated with many [sharp] edges bent towards each other.
<b>Threshing utensils.</b> This is a hacked saw full of cuts like "many sharp edges"<sup>12</sup><i class="footnote">Shmuel I, 13:21.</i> that is called laime (O.F.).
<b>The streets.</b> In the mud of the streets and so Yonason [also] translated: "He dragged them in the streets."
