### passuk 2
<b>With the insignia.</b> Each banner shall have [as] its insignia a colored cloth hanging from it. <i data-commentator="Siftei Chakhamim" data-label="⚬"></i>Each one’s color was not like the other’s, each color corresponded with the hue of its gem set in the breastplate. Thusly, everyone could recognize his banner. Another interpretation [of] “with the insignia of their fathers’ houses”; By the sign their forefather Yaakov gave them <i data-commentator="Siftei Chakhamim" data-label="⚬"></i>when they carried him from Egypt [for burial in Canaan], as it is said, “And his sons did to him just as he had commanded them” [Bereishis, 50:12]. ‘Yehudah, Yissachar, and Zevulun shall carry him from the east, Reuven, Shimon, and Gad from the south; etc.,’ as stated by Tanchuma to this portion. 
<b>At a distance:</b> <i data-commentator="Siftei Chakhamim" data-label="⚬"></i>At the distance of a <i>mil</i> (2,000 <i>amos,</i> approximately 4,000 feet.) as stated in the Book of Joshua [3:4], ‘However, there should be a distance between you and it (the sacred ark) of two thousand <i>amos</i>’, To enable them to come on Shabbos. Moshe and Aharon and his sons and the Levites camped near it (the <i>Mishkon</i>). 

### passuk 3
<b>In the forefront.</b> Toward the side called קֶדֶם, the front. Which is this? <i data-commentator="Siftei Chakhamim" data-label="⚬"></i>This is the eastern side whereas the west is called אָחוֹר, the rear.

### passuk 17
<b>The Tent of Meeting shall travel.</b> <i data-commentator="Siftei Chakhamim" data-label="⚬"></i>After those two banners.
<b>Just as they camp, so shall they travel:</b> As I explained, they traveled as they camped, each banner proceeding in its designated position. 
<b>In his place [literally “his hand”]</b> In his place. Yet, the literal meaning, “his hand” is not out of context. One’s nearby area is called “at hand,” [i.e.,] adjacent, within hand’s reach. <i>en son aise,</i> at hand, in Old French.
