### passuk 6
<b>And cymbals.</b> Targum Yonoson renders, 'and with cymbals,' a type of musical instrument.<sup>1</sup><i class="footnote">Perhaps a three [=<span>שָׁלשׁ</span>] stringed harp.—Radak.</i>

### passuk 7

### passuk 8
<b>He is lacking.</b> What more is he lacking, that he needs to acquire?
<b>Only the kingship.</b> He needs nothing else but the kingship.

### passuk 9
<b>Viewed suspiciously.</b> With an envious glance.

### passuk 10
<b>And he raved.</b> [Literally 'he prophesied'] but Targum renders, 'and he raved.' A prophet and a madman speak in unintelligible hints.

### passuk 11
<b>I will strike Dovid and the wall.</b> I will strike one blow so, that the spear will pierce Dovid and the wall.<sup>2</sup><i class="footnote">Shaul wanted to strike Dovid in a manner that it would appear that it was unintentional.</i>

### passuk 17
<b>Shaul said.</b> To himself.
<b>Let not my hand be upon him.</b> Lest I will be punished.
<b>But let the hand of the Philistines be upon him.</b> Therefore, he said to him, "and fight Adonoy's wars."

### passuk 18

### passuk 19
<b>When the time came to give.</b> When the time arrived which they had set to give her to him, and while they were involved with giving her to him, it turned out that she was given to Adrieil.<sup>4</sup><i class="footnote">Son of Barzili. See II Shmuel 21:8. She married Adrieil without Shaul’s knowledge—Metzudas Dovid.</i>

### passuk 20

### passuk 21
<b>Through the second [of my two daughters] you will become my son-in-law.</b> I have two daughters; through one of them, you will become my son-in-law.<sup>5</sup><i class="footnote">See Maseches Sanhedrin 19b.</i> And similarly did Targum Yonoson render, 'with one of the two.' Similarly [we find], "I hold three things [meaning, one of three things] over you,"<sup>6</sup><i class="footnote">II Shmuel 24:12.</i> as Scripture clarifies there, "choose one of them."<sup>7</sup><i class="footnote">Ibid.</i>
