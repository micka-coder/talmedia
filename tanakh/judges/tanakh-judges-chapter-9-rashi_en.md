### passuk 6
<b> Near the plain of the pillar.</b> "The plain of monuments"—<sup>1</sup><i class="footnote">This is Targum Yonasan’s translation.</i> A plain in which monuments stood.

### passuk 7

### passuk 8
<b> The trees went about resolutely.</b> This is a parable.<sup>2</sup><i class="footnote">Yalkut, 65.</i>
<b>The olive tree.</b> Asniel ben Kenaz,<sup>3</sup><i class="footnote">Above, 3; 9–11. (Yalkut, ibid)</i> a tribesman of Judah, who are entitled "olive", as it is said, "Refreshed olive tree, with fruit beautiful in appearance, etc."<sup>4</sup><i class="footnote">Yirmiyahu, 11:16. The chapter is addressed to “the men of Yehudah” (v.1).</i>

### passuk 9
<b> The richness of my oil.</b> The connotation here is fattiness.

### passuk 10
<b> To the fig tree.</b> This refers to Devorah.<sup>5</sup><i class="footnote"> Above, 4:4. (Yalkut, ibid.)</i>

### passuk 11
<b> My sweetness.</b> the honey of dates.

### passuk 12
<b> To the grapevine.</b> This refers to Gidon,<sup>6</sup><i class="footnote">Yalkut, ibid.</i> a descendant of Yossef, of whom it is said, "A flourishing son"—"like a grapevine positioned, etc."<sup>7</sup><i class="footnote">“A flourishing son over a wellspring”. (Bereishis, 49:22). Targum Onkelos there interprets, “like a grapevine positioned over a wellspring.”</i>

### passuk 13
<b> Which brings joy to God.</b> As the Levi'im chant the hymns of glory only during the wine libation ceremony.<sup>8</sup><i class="footnote">Berachos, 35:b.</i>

### passuk 14
<b> The thornbush.</b> "epine" in old French.

### passuk 28
<b> Who is Avimelech, and who is Shechem.</b> Who is Avimelech to control Shechem, and who is Shechem to be subjugated to Avimelech? Is not Avimelech Yerubaal's son, from Ofros, of the Aviezrites?<sup>10</sup><i class="footnote"></i>
<b>And his henchman Zevul?</b> The city superintendent, named Zevul, is Avimelech's henchman. His master cannot justify his authority over the city; thus his appointee, as well, is a non-entity.
<b>Serve the people of Chamor.</b> If you want to get yourselves leaders, come serve the men of Chamor, who ruled the land in early times.<sup>11</sup><i class="footnote">Bereishis, 34:2.</i>
<b>But why must we serve him?</b> Avimelech.

### passuk 29
<b> He said: For Avimelech.</b> Ga'al declared in the presence of Zevul, "Tell Avimelech."
<b>Beef up your army and come on out</b> from your place, and let us see how grand you are!

### passuk 46
<b> Forest stockade.</b> A forest encircled by twisted trees and branches, called "plisse" in old French.
<b>Of the temple of the god Beris.</b> The stockade belonged to Baal Beris, the name of their deity. Tzalmon<sup>15</sup><i class="footnote">Mentioned in v. 48.</i>, too, owned a forest there. Others interpret <span><b>צֽרִיחַ</b></span> as "voutes" in old French, "bunker," dug into the ground.

### passuk 53
<b> A pestle shard.</b> A fragment of upper grinding stone called "pestle" [lit. "rider.<sup>16</sup><i class="footnote">As the upper pestle stone “rides” the lower mortar stone. (Metzudos).</i>].
<b>And smashed.</b> Crushed.<sup>17</sup><i class="footnote"><span><b>תָּרָץ</b></span> is usually translated as “ran”. Here, however, the translation is “crushed”, or “smashed”.</i>

### passuk 54
<b> Lest it be said of me.</b> Lest it be said concerning me.<sup>18</sup><i class="footnote"><span><b>לי</b></span> is not translated as “to me,” but as “concerning me.”</i>
