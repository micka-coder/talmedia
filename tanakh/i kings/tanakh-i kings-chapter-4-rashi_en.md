
### passuk 1
<b>King over all of Yisroel.</b> When they observed his wisdom they all rejoiced in his kingship.<sup>1</sup><i class="footnote">And no one opposed him.—Radak</i>

### passuk 3
<b>Scribes.</b> To write the [king’s] chronicles.
<b>The secretary.</b> [To record] which legal case came before him first that he might rule on it first.<sup>2</sup><i class="footnote">I.e., he was a court administrator.</i> Thus have I heard.

### passuk 5
<b>Over the officers.</b> He had twelve officers to provide sustenance for the king and his household.

### passuk 6
<b>The tax commissioner.</b> Which he [Shlomo] had raised on [Bnei] Yisroel, as it is stated, “And Shlomo haMelech raised a tax.”<sup>3</sup><i class="footnote">Below 5:27.</i>

### passuk 7
<b>And they provided sustenance for the king.</b> All his meals, and food of the horses and all expenditures for his necessities.<sup>4</sup><i class="footnote">See I Shmuel 8:11-18. There it describes that the king had a right to demand all these things. Shmuel had told the people the king’s rights before he anointed Shaul.</i>
