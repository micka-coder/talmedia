### passuk 10
<b>Male child.</b> [Targum] Yonoson rendered, “one who knows knowledge,” [i.e.,] he who places it into the walls of his heart.<sup>2</sup><i class="footnote">See I Shmuel 25:22.</i><sup>3</sup><i class="footnote">Alternatively, משתין בקיר refers to a dog who urinates on walls, i.e., the prophet is prophesying that the destruction will be so severe, that so much as a dog will not be left when the house of Yerovom is destroyed.—Radak</i>
<b>Any that are restrained and that are abandoned.</b> עצור is one who has someone restraining him, as in, “This one will rule over My people.”<sup>4</sup><i class="footnote">I Shmuel 9:17.</i> And עזוב is one who has no one restraining him.
<b>As one removes.</b> As the one who digests food which [eventually] becomes dung,<sup>5</sup><i class="footnote">Alternatively, גלל means “tooth,” which consumes food completely.—Radak</i> so will I expunge after him until he is totally consumed.<sup>6</sup><i class="footnote">Alternatively, “I will completely remove the house of Yerovom, as one who sweeps out dung until it is thoroughly cleaned out.—Metzudas Dovid</i>

### passuk 13
<b>Something good.</b> He abandoned his watch; for his father had stationed him as a sentry so that no person would go up [to Yerusholayim] on the pilgrimage festival. He abandoned his watch and went up.<sup>7</sup><i class="footnote">See Maseches Mo’ed Katan 28b.</i>

### passuk 14
<b>Who will cut off the house of Yerovom this day and also those that will be born.</b> Those who are already born to him this day, and those who are destined to be born to him from now on, so did [Targum] Yonoson render.

### passuk 25
<b>Shishak.</b> We find in Midrash Shir Hashirim that this was Pharaoh Neco. He was called Shishak because all his life he longed for [=שוקק] the ivory throne that belonged to Shlomo his son-in-law, and now, he went up and took it.

### passuk 26
<b>He took everything.</b> The throne, that was dearer to him than everything.
